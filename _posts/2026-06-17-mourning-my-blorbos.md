I don't know where to start... I finished the novel; there was a game jam...<br><br>

Alright, let's start with The Snake Queen. Two days ago I wrote the final chapter and the epilogue. I still need to edit them and prepare them for publishing, but you should be able to access them soon. It made me feel very sad, and I will tell you why.

I first came up with the idea for this story when I was about 16. I spent long years trying to make webcomics, and I wasted a lot of time restarting one single storyline, which prevented me from even getting anywhere close to The Snake Queen. Raajimera existed as an OC that I sometimes drew and had a backstory for, but I didn't have the energy or focus to work on her properly.

Due to some traumatizing events that made me scared of writing, I spent all this time running away from the possibility of writing a story. I tried to force myself into comics because there the prose doesn't have to be stellar, and I could just focus on the dialogue.

That was until 2023, I believe, when I met some people in World of Warcraft, and one of them was deeply in love with amateur writing. I told her I can't write because English is not my native language, and she told me that it's not a problem because people would love it anyway as long as the story is cool.

Somewhere around 2024 I decided to start the first draft of The Snake Queen, but it was super rough because I still had to figure out how in heaven I'm even supposed to write prose in English. Those of you who had ever seen how novels written in European languages are formatted will know exactly how big the difference is.

In March 2025 I decided to redraft the entire project, applying all the things I learned during the first draft. I went from 18k words in total to over 50k, officially hitting the minimum requirement for it to be considered a novel and making it book-sized.

I had a lot of fun going through all these adventures with Raajimera, Zephyr, and Feng despite the fact that she was never my favorite OC prior to The Snake Queen. I really got into their storyline, and finishing it made me upset because it meant no more chapters like these. I can still make mini comics and extra content with them, but it will not be the same. I mourned my three little weirdos as if I just ended a real relationship.

I really love all three of them.

After publishing the final part of the novel, I will probably organize an event to celebrate. Once the event is over, I will begin working on the illustrated version of The Snake Queen, which will be sold on my Ko-fi page and possibly on itch.io. I wanted to make a special version that people could buy if they liked the free one and wanted to support me.

At the same time I will begin my next novel, Little Star and the City of Wonders. I hope you will enjoy that one too!<br><br>

Onto the next topic I go... Last week I attended a local game jam with my partner. We created a quick little game called Vault of the Time Mage (for which I created characters and background). The game is in Spanish, but we will add English too. Eventually.

You can look it up on <a href="https://juanjosalvador.itch.io/vault-of-the-time-mage">itch.io</a>

The game is about a silly time mage who has guests from all over the timeline, and he tries to create custom items for them out of random stuff he has in his vault. My partner hopes to make a proper version and release the demo by the end of the month. He is super excited about it, and I can't wait to help him with the rest of the assets.<br><br>

Another thing I wanted to say before this blog entry ends is that today I have officially quit Side7. I wiped my gallery in there and logged out. I spent a long time wondering if I should do it; however, there are certain people in there I don't want to be associated with, so I decided to just pull the plug before the novel releases. I will miss some of the people I met there, but I feel like I just don't want to be there anymore in its current state.

I hope they won't hold it against me.
