Zephyr led Raajimera to a shady street that seemed to be abandoned. It looked much less glamorous than everything she saw until now. The sad little houses were giving her chills, but at least there were no guards around to stop them.

They climbed on top of a wall that led them to something akin to a watchtower.

The man pointed at a big building ahead of them.

"This is the emperor's palace," he said. "There is a blind spot that we can use to sneak inside."

Raajimera remembered this part of the plan. She nodded.

"This is where I give us different disguises," she whispered.

The woman took a closer look at the palace. It was a true marvel of architecture. The building looked like it was built atop water, surrounded with trees and smaller plants. In front of the entrance, there were two rounded platforms connected with bridges, creating a clear pathway from the beginning of the lake to the door of the palace.

"The emperor always gives a speech there, on the big platform," Zephyr said. "He did this before every wedding he had. The groom is not supposed to see the bride before the ceremony, but he spits in the face of tradition to humiliate them into submission."

Raajimera looked at the walls surrounding the palace. She figured out he would let the people in to witness his speech, but his home was conveniently separated from the poverty of the district they found themselves in. Everything he did seemed performative.

They climbed back down and waltzed through the district undetected. After a while of walking, Zephyr stopped in front of the wall that separated the palace garden from the rest of the city. He gently pushed one brick and revealed a poorly disguised hole.

"We can enter through here," he said. "This hole has been here for years. They tried to fix it plenty of times, but there is a reason why it stays this way."

Raajimera looked at him and asked, "Does that reason have curly brown hair and trembling hands?"

Zephyr looked her dead in the eye and responded, "You will never know."

They quietly snuck in. The man was right; nobody could see them from this position because the wall that faced the hole didn't have any windows on it.

Zephyr and Raajimera quietly approached the back door. They could hear the imperial cooks working on the other side.

The woman took Feng out of her shawl.

"Can you help us?" she asked quietly, not to raise any suspicion.

Feng looked at her with sleepy eyes and yawned. The animal clearly missed the entire journey up until this point.

"What do you want me to do?" it asked with dizziness painted on its face.

"Could you sneak into the building and see if the road is clear for us to enter?" Raajimera said.

Feng jumped down and slid under the door.

They suddenly heard a group of women screaming and running out of the kitchen in fear.

Feng returned to Raajimera and said, "Now it is."

Zephyr gently opened the back door. They took a look inside. The corridor seemed empty.

They quietly went inside and took a glance through the open kitchen door. All the cooks left, but it seemed like two guards decided to take advantage of the situation and eat whatever these poor terrified women left behind when they were fleeing from Feng.

Zephyr took a rolling pin from the counter and silently got behind the munching guards. He quickly hit them in the backs of the heads, and they dropped unconscious without even realizing what got them.

The man gestured at Raajimera to let her know he will need help. He placed the rolling pin back where he found it, and they dragged one of the guards across the corridor into a relatively small storage room on the opposite side. Then they placed the second guard next to the first one.

The chamber was big enough to let them lock themselves in it along with their victims, but most of the space was taken by food products and kitchen utensils.

"Zephyr, there is a problem here," Raajimera whispered.

"What is it?" he asked equally silently.

"I was supposed to copy their appearance, but I don't know what the color of their eyes is," she responded.

Zephyr sighed. He knelt down and gently peeled their eyelids open with his fingertips.

"Gray and black," he finally said.

Raajimera nodded, trying to absorb as much of their faces as she could. She then took two of her enchanted flowers out and imbued them with the illusions.

Zephyr observed her in awe.

The woman handed him one of the flowers and said, "Hold it in your hand and wait until the illusion appears in front of you. Then walk through it and hide this item in your pocket."

They both applied their disguises.

Zephyr took a moment to tie up the incapacitated guards. He searched them before leaving. One of the guards had a note on him with the location and time.

"It must be where they were supposed to rejoin the emperor right before the speech," he said and put the note in his pocket. They left the storage room, locking the door behind them.

Raajimera took a look at the corridor. The entire palace looked very regal and rich. Beautiful chandeliers were hanging from tall ceilings like a million shards caught in a net. The walls were covered with artworks separated by long red curtains.

They were walking through this beautiful space when she saw Zephyr stop briefly in front of one of the long wall mirrors. He marveled at his disguise, then said, "Incredible, it looks so real. Where have you been all my life?"

His voice was so low. It was as if he didn't want her to hear him, but she did.

Raajimera felt as if she was about to blush under her disguise, but she tried not to get distracted from their objective.

They arrived at the middle of the building, where long spiral stairs crept downwards, connecting the floors together. There was a table covered with decorative items between them.

Soft daylight was shining from the small decorative window above the staircase. The stained glass was making this room look incredibly beautiful.

Zephyr grabbed a pike from a weapon's rack, and Raajimera did the same. They stood next to the right side of the stairs. Quickly enough, two other guards appeared on the left side and just stood there straight as a broomstick.

She thought nothing could stain this picturesque palace. Everything was just so beautiful around here.

Then she saw **him**.

There he was, a man leading his bride downstairs. She looked like she was taken hostage; he just towed her.

His face looked distorted. He had a large crooked nose, pointy cheekbones, and dark shadows under his small eyes. The shape of his face made him look like he must have been stuck screaming for one thousand years before he learned to close his mouth.

Zephyr really didn't lie when he said the emperor looked like a fiend from the underworld.

The bride was wearing a beautiful floral silk gown and a matching veil on her hair. However, nothing could distract Raajimera from her unhappy expression. She felt so sorry for her.

The emperor and his woman walked down ceremonially. His head was up, hers was down. He gestured at the guards to follow him, and the show began.

They followed them across the corridor to the main entrance of the palace. The massive door opened with a squeak, and Raajimera saw the crowd of people gathering around the platforms just as she imagined they would.

The emperor's overwhelming presence made everyone feel smaller. He truly was a terrifying person.

The guests were watching him take his position.

The emperor walked to the middle of the big platform, dragging his bride with him.

Raajimera, Zephyr, and the other two guards followed him and stopped behind him.

"People of Hazyan city," the emperor said, "I present you my new bride!"

He pulled her in front of himself. The woman tried not to break down in front of the crowd.

"May this ceremony run with blood for our gods; may they bless this empire and us with it," the emperor exclaimed.

Raajimera used her powers to summon snakes from the surrounding waters. The animals were hardwired to answer Raa's call. They crept out of the lake around the platforms, ready to strike the emperor, just as they did back in Duskvale to protect Raajimera.

Suddenly the bride saw them in the corner of her eye and panicked. She was terrified of snakes.

Without missing a beat, the emperor turned around and cast what appeared to be a projectile of dark matter at his guards. They jumped away, but Raajimera dropped her flower while dodging, revealing her real form.

He was about to attack her. Seeing the fury in his eyes, Raajimera realized that she will not stand a chance unless she succumbs to the darkness in her heart that she spent so long dodging. She didn't have time to consider it; she attempted to bind herself to the water the same way as she did back in Duskvale.

Suddenly the world around her disappeared; she was surrounded with bright energy nodes resembling stars. She saw her water clone once more.

They looked at each other.

_You are back_, the clone said, _hold my hand. Nothing bad can happen to you now._

Raajimera extended her hand, and their fingers intertwined. She could feel something shift in her. Deeply repressed dark urges hit her at full intensity.

The woman snapped out of the vision and quickly counterattacked the emperor's spell with water. She wiped small droplets from her face, revealing the mark that adorned her forehead.

She could see the emperor recognized it.

He didn't hold back. He knew exactly who he was dealing with.

Raajimera managed to hold her ground for long enough, but the longer the fight took, the more powerful he seemed.

She tried to distract him with illusions, but magic from the underworld was difficult to counter.

Zephyr, still disguised as a guard, watched her dodge increasingly stronger shadow spells. Realizing she might not keep her feet on the ground for long, he decided to give her a small helping hand.

Taking advantage of the ensuing chaos, he moved behind the emperor and blew the ornate horn he brought with him.

The sinister howl pierced the air. Unsure where the sound came from, the guests flinched in fear.

For a split second the emperor was disoriented. Raajimera noticed his confusion and engulfed the lower half of his body with water, which she then rapidly froze. She conjured large icicles and launched them forward, piercing him right through the chest.

Seeing him in pain did nothing to her. She swung the temperature from icy to boiling hot.

The man fell to the ground.

His terrified bride was still there, frozen in fear and unable to move.

"Run, this isn't about you," Raajimera exclaimed at her.

The poor woman ran for her life towards the city.

Raajimera lifted her hands up and forced dark clouds to gather in the sky.

"Raa, Spirit of Dusk! Hear me!" she shouted. "Behold this empire I conquered for you!"

She didn't know where she knew these words from. They still were ingrained on the back of her head.

Despite the time, the Twilight Mother appeared in the sky and hissed ominously.

The crowd of people started to flee in panic. Raajimera couldn't hear anything over the sounds of their screams.

A group of guards tried to stop her, but she had no issue holding them off.

Meanwhile, Zephyr approached the dying emperor. He knelt above him to check if he was still breathing.

The monarch looked up and grabbed Zephyr's arm.

"It's you...now I can see through this specific flavor of magic," he said, visibly struggling. "I wanted to give you a new life, to make you one of us. Yet you brought the serpent right into my home. Now you will have to live in the doomed world you have created."

The emperor's grasp weakened; his hand fell to the ground.

Zephyr gently closed the monarch's eyes with his fingertips.

"Rest in peace," he said and got up, ready to walk away. He then saw Raajimera hitting herself in the forearm in rabid fury. She squealed and grabbed her wrist.

The woman felt something under her fingers, so she looked under her sleeve. It was the bracelet she got from Zephyr and Feng.

"This isn't me," she said to herself as she dropped to her knees. The dark influence ended and was replaced by the feeling of hopelessness. She was on the verge of tears.

Zephyr looked around and saw the other guards around her, but they were visibly terrified of her. He walked to her, ceremonially placed his weapon on the ground, and knelt in front of them to give them an example.

"I felt like I was possessed," Raajimera admitted quietly.

Zephyr leaned towards her and said, "Then pretend you still are; don't lose the momentum, or they could turn against us."

She looked up and saw all the guards kneeling in front of her with their weapons on the ground.

Raajimera decided to pretend she was still cold and powerful.

"You there," she shouted and pointed at one of the guards. "Where are the prisoners who were supposed to be sacrificed today?"

The man stood up visibly shaken from everything that happened.

"In a dungeon not too far from here," he responded.

"Take your God-Empress there," she demanded.

The guards obeyed and led Raajimera to the chariot that used to belong to the emperor. They went towards the dungeon.

Raajimera looked up and could still see Raa in the sky. She looked terrifying from this angle.

The woman looked around. This beautiful, lively city she saw earlier was quiet. Doors and windows were closed shut; she could still see some eyes on her hiding in the shadows.

They finally arrived at the building where the prisoners were kept. It looked horrible compared to the rest of the city. The beautiful architecture was nowhere to be found. In front of her there was just a small stone construction with bars for a door.

The gate opened and she saw a dark underground corridor. Their steps caused a big panic in the cells.

"The emperor is here!" the prisoners were shouting.

Raajimera went out of the shadows.

"The emperor is dead," she said. "Now it's only me."

The prisoners went silent. They were watching their new empress in disbelief.

Raajimera went to the middle, appearing very regal. She looked at the cells and recognized the people from Duskvale. She approached them.

"Snake Queen!" Abbas shouted, trying to pretend they are still friends, "You conquered the empire for us!"

"For you!?" she raised her voice. "I conquered it for Raa. You have no place here."

His face went from a forced smile to fear in this one second.

"Zephyr, open this cell," she said.

"Right away, _dear_," he responded as he took the key from another guard.

He stopped in front of the cell and took his enchanted flower out. He rapidly smashed it against the stone floor of the dungeon, dropping his disguise. He then removed the hood from his head to make sure the residents of Duskvale saw his face.

"_You_ are Zephyr?" Abbas said in disbelief, "I thought you were dead."

"Wouldn't that make it easy for you?" Zephyr answered as he unlocked the door.

Abbas looked at them both, and a sudden realization hit him. He figured that there was nothing he could have said to turn this situation around since they already knew each other.

"Considering what you did to both of us, you are not allowed to stay on the imperial territory," Raajimera said, her tone still serious. "Your village is gone; you have to find a new place to call home. There is no redemption for you, but your children still have a chance. Teach them how to be better people, but be aware that now I am the empire and the serpent is on the prowl."

The people of Duskvale silently left the dungeon, escorted out by two guards.

Raajimera looked around and saw that there was a small child in one of the cells. She approached the little person and said, "Give me your hand."

The little girl hesitated but obeyed the empress.

Raajimera closed her eyes—her mark began shining.

"You don't belong here," she said as she ordered the guards to release the girl.

The woman opened her eyes and looked at all the other prisoners.

"None of you do," she stated. "Guards, release these people and bring them outside. I want none of the prisoners mistreated!"

The dungeon started emptying out until the only people remaining in it were Raajimera and Zephyr.

"To think I hoped you would return to the Howling Pass with me once this is all over," he said. "You are perfect for this role, and these people really need you."

Raajimera looked at him and said, "Actually, I hoped you would stay here with me...as my consort."

Zephyr lifted his eyebrows.

"Are you asking me to marry you?"

Raajimera looked him deep in the eye and responded, "Yes. Yes, I am."

"You know, ever since I first saw you, I was ready to just help you and let you go," he said. "You are divine in every meaning of that word, and I am just...you can see me. Not to mention, I spent so much time living in the shadows that now it would be really difficult for me to have it any other way."

Raajimera was trying to hide the disappointment in her face. He could see her act slip a bit as she said, "You can return to the Howling Pass if that is what you wish."

Zephyr shook his head.

"Of course not," he responded. "That would be the biggest mistake of my life. There would always be a Raajimera-shaped hole in my heart that I would never be able to fill. I just want you to know that it will not be easy for me."

Raajimera's face brightened. She hugged him tight.

Zephyr gently kissed her forehead, knowing very well that it was unwise to be so close to her mark again.

She released him and looked at his face. He seemed happy, but she didn't expect to be able to see it.

"We will have to go there and talk to the prisoners," she finally said.

"In a while," he responded and gave her a little kiss.

She grabbed his hand.

"Let's go," she said. "One more thing, though. We will have to fix that hole."

Zephyr seemed slightly embarrassed.

"You're right," he said. "I don't think the reason will have any use of it anymore."

She laughed and led him out of the dungeon.

Raa was still in the sky, but now the crowd was too preoccupied with their newfound freedom to pay attention to her.

The moment Raajimera and Zephyr appeared on the surface again, the prisoners surrounded them shouting over each other.

"Empress! My husband is in another dungeon!"

"My son is about to be sacrificed next week!"

Raajimera raised her hand up to silence them.

"Nobody will be sacrificed anymore," she shouted. "The goddess only demands your worship. She has no use for your blood."

The prisoners looked at the sky. Raa nodded.

The empress pointed at one of the guards.

"You there," she said. "I want you to send out the message to every corner of the empire. Tell them to release their prisoners and transport them here where they can face the divine judgment. Then I want you and the other guards to escort them back into their homes and treat them with the respect they deserve."

The prisoners began cheering and extending their hands to touch Raajimera.

Suddenly one of them asked, "Empress, who is this man?"

Raajimera looked at Zephyr and tightened her hand around his.

"This is my betrothed," she finally said.

"Zephyr of the Howling Pass," he added.

The crowd fell silent. They looked at him as if they saw a ghost.

"The legend is real?" someone asked. "I thought it was just a story."

Everyone wanted to see him, which made him deeply uncomfortable. Raajimera and Zephyr couldn't even move surrounded by all these people.

Suddenly Raa spoke from the sky, "Please, let my followers rest. I will be here until sunrise to answer all of your questions."

Raa pointed at one of the guards with her tail.

"You there, lead them back to the palace and show them their room," she ordered.

The guard was too terrified to oppose a literal goddess, so he led Raajimera and Zephyr back into the chariot that brought them here.

The woman looked back as they left. She could see the citizens and the prisoners mix trying to get Raa's attention.

Raajimera was so happy that the goddess freed them like this. The sheer size of the crowd was overwhelming, and she felt exhausted after the whole day. She placed her head on Zephyr's shoulder and closed her eyes. He put his hand on hers and squeezed it.

The chariot finally stopped, and they could walk to the palace. The garden seemed slightly demolished, and it was now their problem.

The guard led them inside and ordered the handmaidens to prepare the bedroom for the new empress and her future consort.

They waited in the same room where just several hours ago Raajimera saw the emperor for the first time. She couldn't believe that it was already done and this man would never threaten them again.

When the room was finally prepared for them, they shyly entered and locked the door.

Their new bedroom was very spacious and had large windows out of which they could see the city in the setting sun. The furniture was large and ornate. The entire chamber was filled with cushions, plants, and golden decorations. The most impressive item in the room was a large bed with a strong wooden frame covered with carvings.

Raajimera freed herself from the outfit she was wearing and threw herself on the bed in only her underwear. Zephyr did the same, and they were just lying down next to each other and looking at the beautiful purple canopy above them.

The bed sheets were very soft and fresh. It felt like a completely new experience to her after so many weeks spent in bare minimum.

"We did it..." the man finally said and looked at her.

"Indeed. Why didn't you present yourself under your real name in front of the prisoners, though?" she asked.

He took a deep breath.

"My old life is over, but it will make it much easier for you to rule over this place when the citizens are aware that your husband has the secrets of half of the empire and knows how to find out the other half," he finally said.

"Don't you miss your old name?" she asked.

"No, I am Zephyr now," he responded, "and that's who I want to be until the end."

She looked at him and realized that this was how she felt about her current name as well.

Raajimera curled up against him, and he hugged her. He noticed that she now had a bruise on her forearm.

"You have to be more careful," he said. "I can't keep patching you up forever!"

Raajimera laughed and started passionately kissing him.

Zephyr stopped her for a moment and asked, "Where is Feng?"

She looked at him and replied, "I don't know. Feng will find us at some point. It always does."

"Fair point," he said, and they picked up where they left off.

Feng found itself atop one of the towers of the Hazyan city. As the night fell, the snake lifted its little snout into the sky.

"That went rather well, didn't it?" it said.

Raa looked at the animal with a warm expression.

"Indeed," she responded. "The champion made great progress; it's good to see her succeed."

"I am proud of her," Feng said.

"So am I, but I have to tell you that I was a little surprised today."

"What do you mean?" the animal asked.

"When I put him on her way, I expected him to simply bring her here," Raa responded. "I didn't think they would even like each other given their backgrounds. Then I heard they decided to get married."

Raa looked at Feng with an amused expression.

"I am happy for them, however," she added.

"Me too. He is a little feral, but he treats her right," Feng stated.

Raa nodded.

Feng glanced at her and said, "I think I will look for them."

"It's late; they are probably asleep," Raa stated.

"Then I will sleep between them!"

The spirit laughed and said, "Oh, little friend, never change."
