The village was full of clamor as its inhabitants worked like a colony of ants. It has been three days since the traveling merchant warned them about an impending attack from the Empire. They had to raise walls around the settlements, as they speculated that this attack would be unlike anything that had ever happened to them. 

The chieftain closely monitored the construction process when Raajimera approached him. 

He turned around and looked at her without a word. She was the only person who wasn't helping with the construction, which vexed him a little. 

"Chief!" she spoke. "I think I have found a way to protect the village, but…"

"What is it?" he asked. The closer she was, the clearer she could see the bags under his eyes. The poor man probably didn't sleep last night.

"I will need help from the entire village. It will just take a moment—"

The chieftain interrupted her. "We cannot afford even one minute of a delay," he said. "If you want to help us, then pick up a hammer or whatever you're comfortable using and aid them."

Raajimera didn't know what to say, but she felt like her idea was the only correct approach to this problem, and she wasn't going to give up so easily.

"Chieftain, I assure you that my idea will give this village much better protection than even the tallest walls!" The woman raised her voice unintentionally. "I intend to use my powers to create a protective barrier that will repel the raiders once and for all."

"Raajimera, I appreciate what you did for us so far; I really do," the chieftain said, "but we require a reliable way of protecting the village. Are you sure that your idea will aid us?"

"Chief, your village prayed to Raa to bring me here and help you," Raajimera explained. "I swear on her name that it will work."

The village leader sighed heavily and said with a defeated expression, "Fine. Ask my people for help, but if your plan won't work, you really will need to assist them before we run out of time."

"You have my word," she said. The woman put her hand on her heart as a sign of a promise and quickly left to ask the villagers to join her. 

The people of Duskvale hesitantly left their workplaces and gathered around the well with her. 

Raajimera already pre-positioned her flowers all around the settlement. She checked on them one last time to make sure that nothing happened while she was away and returned to the villagers crowded around the well.

The woman cleared her throat and gave them a speech.

"Dear friends," she said, "today I will give you the most precious gift of all. I will protect you as you protected me."

Bewildered villagers looked at each other. 

"The power bestowed upon me by Raa allows me to weave a protective barrier above the village," Raajimera explained. "Duskvale will disappear to the evil eye; we will live here and prosper as the rest of the world shivers in the emperor's sight." She paused for a moment, then continued, "However, I am still weak, and I will need you to lend me your life energy to power such a grand spell."

She heard the familiar voice of Aya from the crowd. 

"Is it going to hurt us?" the girl asked.

Raajimera locked eyes with her and responded, "No, not at all. You will get your energy back once the spell is in place."

_Or at least I hope so_, the demigoddess thought to herself.

"I thought you would need to sacrifice us," one of the village men said with a hint of distress in his voice.

"That wouldn't make any sense," Raajimera answered. "I just need you all to join hands in a circle with me. I promise nothing bad will happen."

The locals reluctantly agreed and tentatively grabbed each other's hands, forming a giant circle around the well. 

Raajimera grabbed the nearly empty sack of seeds and theatrically threw the rest of them onto the surrounding ground. She had already planted most of them the night before, but she intended to show her people that they had nothing to be afraid of. 

The woman threw the empty sack on the ground behind her and joined the circle, grabbing the chieftain's hand. 

"This day marks the beginning of the new glorious era, one of abundance and happiness, without fear of the empire," she said and raised both her hands; the people followed her example. "Beautiful foliage will turn this place into an oasis for generations to come, as the water in the well will never dry out." 

The woman made a short pause and shouted, "May this blessing of Raa forever protect this village!"

All of a sudden, the ground shook and burst with beautiful foliage of all shades of green. Vivid flowers bloomed right before their eyes. The branches of the freshly grown trees bent under the weight of scrumptious fruits of all kinds. 

The villagers couldn't believe their eyes. Right around them, the ground lit up, and a golden ring of celestial energy emerged and began wandering up the sky. It shone brightly until it shrank and disappeared on top of what was now a dome above the blessed oasis. 

"What is this?" Aya, the young village lady, asked, visibly awestruck. She wanted to rub her eyes in disbelief, but she wasn't sure if she could let go of the hands of the people next to her yet. 

Raajimera appeared incredibly regal. She looked at the villagers and released their hands from her grasp. "I created a protection spell that will hide the village from the outside," she said, her voice full of pride. "The barrier will be kept up indefinitely, sustained by the life force of the foliage within it.

The chieftain was floored by all he saw. "How is this possible?" he asked. "Can anyone check it?"

One of the village men ventured to the edge of the dome. He spent a moment jumping from one side to another and then returned. The man turned to the chieftain and said, "It really does work as she claims. See it with your own eyes; I've never seen anything like it."

"Incredible!" the chieftain shouted and clapped his hands together. "I'm impressed."

Raajimera smiled and exclaimed, "See? Raa is on our side! With her blessings—"

"We still require you to pick your Snake King," the chieftain interrupted her sharply. His face suddenly shifted from warm and amused to cold and calculated.

The woman lost the smile on her face. "I've just shown you that I am more than capable of taking care of the village on my own," she protested.

The chieftain started, "The prophecy—" but Raajimera interrupted him suddenly. 

"According to it, the village would only be safe once the Snake King is selected, but it already is protected without one," she stated. "Moreover, you never told me how the prophecy ends. Why is it even necessary?"

She remained defensive.

The chieftain rolled his eyes and stayed unconvinced. "You already know precisely what you need to know. It is imperative that you choose the Snake King now."

"Perhaps I would, but I don't even know the candidates well enough." Raajimera crossed her arms and made a grumpy face. "Only one of them was thrown in my face at all times while I felt like I wasn't allowed to talk to the remaining ones. I am not convinced that is fair."

Attempts to reason with this man appeared to be fruitless. He listened to her like a rock would, maybe even less so.

"Then pick that one!" he burst out. "You say you can protect us on your own; why does it matter who assists you?

Raajimera tried to remain in the conversation, but she felt like the brute force of the man's stone-hard head was pushing her out.

"I just—" she said.

"You sound ungrateful. I can assure you that he was following you around because he cares about this village as deeply as you do," he snapped.

That sentence pulled some strings in her. How dare he call her ungrateful after all these moments she helped them and they didn't seem to pay her any mind?

Raajimera wanted to continue arguing when she noticed that everyone's eyes were on her. She realized that in this state it would be too easy for her to unintentionally insult them, and that wasn't her goal. The woman took a deep breath and looked at the crowd. Their eyes seemed confused.

_They don't deserve it_, she thought to herself.

The prophecy seemed to be an important part of their culture, the culture she didn't entirely understand. They accepted her as one of them, and they follow the same animal god. The weight of their eyes pinning her down was too much for her to bear. She realized that he might be the one in the wrong, and she should respect their customs.

Seyed approached her. The way he looked at her was so sweet, but she still felt betrayed by his unknowingly revealed face.

Perhaps it was also because she envisioned herself as the sole savior of Duskvale. Raajimera remembered the last time Feng scolded her due to her ego. She didn't want it to get the best of her.

Seyed seemed to be reading her mind. "It will be alright; this is merely a formality," he said.

She took a deep breath. He was right. She could still be considered a hero, even if somebody assisted her.

_Maybe that was my lesson? To accept help?_ she thought to herself.

Raajimera turned towards him, struggling to retain her composure in the situation.

"Seyed," she said, "for your exceptional participation in my endeavors, I have chosen you as the snake king."

It felt wrong, but it wasn't supposed to change anything. She tried to think of it as a political choice rather than a personal one.

Seyed's face brightened even more. He appeared excited. The man grabbed her hands firmly and looked right into her eyes, saying, "I shall protect you until the end, Milady."

The chieftain cut between them and exclaimed, "This calls for a celebration!" as he encircled them with his arms, applying more pressure than she expected him to. "Let's arrange a coronation party worthy of real royalty!"

"Another one?" Raajimera asked, trying to grasp for air. "I thought you said we must prepare for an attack. Do you think this is the right time for that?"

The leader's hand tightened its grip on her shoulder. "We are protected, are we not?" he asked harshly.

"That's correct," she answered after a moment of hesitation. Raajimera didn't know what to make out of this interaction.

The chieftain released them and shouted towards the crowd, "Let's party!"

Everyone applauded and gathered around Raajimera and Seyed. They almost seemed like they wanted to carry these two on their hands. Everything was happening so rapidly. Raajimera felt like she started to phase out.

Before she knew it, the entire village was decorated with flowers and small trinkets. Loud music filled the whole settlement, and the smell of freshly grown plants mixed with the scent of scrumptious food.

Aya and other young village ladies put some flowers in her hair and led her in front of a beautiful decorative arch. They had two intricate diadems on red pillows with them, and they intended to gift them to their saviors.

The chieftain approached the floral arch from the opposite side and gestured to Raajimera and Seyed to take their places there. He took one of the diadems and smiled. "With these crowns," he said, "we officially recognize you two as Snake Queen and Snake King." The man put the first diadem on Raajimera's head and then the other one on Seyed's.

Everyone cheered and began to dance. It was a really joyful moment for each one of them.

Seyed offered Raajimera his hand, and they quickly joined the rest on the dance floor. He looked at her and said, "You look incredible." The man sighed. "The flowers in your hair and the reflection of light in your eyes…"

Raajimera couldn't decide what to say. She looked away for a moment and then mumbled, "Thank you."

"Milady, there is a small secret that we have been keeping from you," he said without a change in his voice.

The woman felt like a spike went through her stomach. She started to wonder if she would finally hear the end of their prophecy. Her eyes looked at him, silently asking him a thousand questions, while her lips said none.

"The entire village prepared a gift for you for the day when you finally prove that you are the real Snake Queen," he said. "I want you to see it before the end of the party."

"Why?" Raajimera said as she finally regained the voice in her throat.

"As your second, I want to make sure that you know how to react when you get it," Seyed explained. "I know how difficult it is for you to find yourself among us; trust me, despite everything, I am the same."

He grabbed her hands once more, this time in a more tender way. "I want you to feel comfortable. When they finally reveal it, you will know exactly what to say."

Raajimera felt grateful for the life she was given in Duskvale. She felt like she should put more faith in her people, yet there was something rehearsed about the way he said it.

The woman looked at the chieftain and saw him with a grin on his face. She glanced at the crowd. The music was playing, and everyone was smiling; she didn't want to be the person who ruined it.

"Alright," she finally said. "Lead me to it."

The man softly held her hand and slowly went away from the heart of the party. The music started sounding quieter and quieter with each step. They found themselves in front of a small building she had never noticed until now. It was hidden on the outskirts of the village.

Raajimera looked away for a moment and saw the horizon from behind the protective barrier. The world seemed so peaceful.

Seyed unlocked the door and proceeded inside. Raajimera followed him. Everything in the room was covered with fabric of a dusty color.

"This is one of our storage rooms," Seyed explained. "I'm sorry about the mess."

Raajimera looked around. She noticed a small window on the wall, but it was blocked with what appeared to be a piece of linen cloth. Judging by the smell, the little building has been locked for a long while.

Seyed looked at her and said, "I will uncover your gift, but I would rather not spoil it for you before you can see it fully."

The man gestured at the room and requested, "Can you close your eyes and turn around until I am done? I promise you it's worth the wait."

"I'm not sure..." she nearly whispered.

"Relax," Seyed responded warmly. "Everything is as it should be."

Raajimera nodded and turned around hesitantly. The woman could hear faint noises in the background. She tried to distract herself by paying attention to the sounds of the village.

Suddenly, something fell out of her hidden pocket and hit her leg.

_Oh no, I forgot about Feng!_ she thought to herself. _I think the poor thing fell down and maybe even broke a bone._

Raajimera forgot about the request and turned around to collect her scaly friend from the ground. She would probably see a snake's fang if the sharpest thing she witnessed wasn't an ornate ritual blade aimed where her back was exactly one second ago.

Seyed held on to the handle of the dagger as if his life depended on it. Behind the man, there was the same mysterious box with a painting of a snake that she found in the chieftain's house.

She gasped and nearly tripped over, desperately trying to retreat.

"What?" she asked, incapable of hiding the fear in her voice.

However, her answer would never come, as the man thrust the blade towards her and said, "I will bask in eternal glory as I challenge the gods after drawing your blood, _Queen_."

She tried to jump away, but the dagger opened a small wound on her chest, dealing her great pain. The woman didn't know what the meaning of this was. She was supposed to be immortal; why would a mere blade hurt her?

The man pushed her to the ground and attempted to finish the job. She struggled against him; she held his wrists, preventing him from stabbing her, but he was much stronger than she was.

Suddenly wild snakes appeared from all sides and attacked him. The vicious beasts attacked in a blind fit of rage. Seyed quickly realized that he can't do anything against the overwhelming force of nature. He struggled against the scaly ambush, but the animals were relentless. Some of them left him with painful bites, whereas others coiled around his limbs until he could no longer feel them.

The man put his last bits of energy towards wielding the blade, but shortly after the ornate dagger fell to the ground with a loud clink. Seyed's lifeless body followed shortly after.

Raajimera's legs were shaking, yet she couldn't seem to move them. She felt paralyzed by the situation.

The animals encircled her yet dealt her no harm. It was as if they were waiting for an order. She took a deep breath and extended her hand towards them.

One of the animals gently put its head on her palm and stuck its tongue out. Raajimera looked the animal in the eyes and realized they were there on Raa's behalf.

The woman grabbed the dagger and tightened her grip around it. She tilted the blade and touched it with her fingertips. The weapon was incredibly sharp. She looked at the man who had just attempted to assassinate her and felt a sudden surge of hatred and disgust towards not only him but also the rest of the village.

Her fear subsided and was replaced with increasing anger.

Raajimera slowly walked out of the small room, leaving Seyed's body behind. She pressed her hand to her wound to try to stop the bleeding. She was not sure whether the injury burned her more than the weight of their betrayal.

Everyone was waiting outside, clearly expecting to see Seyed. When they noticed that it was Raajimera still convulsively holding the dagger in her other hand, they somehow got even paler, physically transgressing the limits of their natural color palette.

The snakes slithered right behind her in a protective manner, measuring the village people with caviling stares.

The woman made eye contact with the villagers. "You knew," she said with judgmental intent. Her voice initially sounded sorrowful, but it increasingly became more and more infuriated. "All of you."

"Queen, we—" the chieftain tried to say something, but suddenly his face was distorted in fear.

"Enough with your fake titles!" she yelled.

Raajimera's eyes began to glow as her forehead mark shifted to white. In a split second, her face seemed so otherworldly, as if she were replaced by a thoroughly different person. They would quickly find out why…

The villagers suddenly heard Raa's voice coming from her mouth. It was loud and primal. A cold chill ran down their spines; they had never heard the voice of their goddess before.

"Rats, all of you!" the Twilight Mother thundered. Her voice, like a million shards that adorn her body. "You begged me for a champion to save your pitiful little village. Now you will beg for mercy as I wipe your pathetic settlement off the surface of this world!"

The inhabitants of Duskvale frantically tried to move away from Raa's wrath. 

"I ought to witness you fall, one by one, for what you have done. See your precious little village crumble to dust, just as you have tried to destroy my beloved champion." Raa hissed ominously.

The inhabitants of Duskvale held their breaths.

"For now, however, I shall let her decide what to do with you. Should you be forgiven or perish, it's all in her hands," the celestial animal continued, "You can only hope for her to be better than I am, as now, I shall no longer answer your prayers, and I shall not stop her should she decide to end your pitiful existence."

As Raa's influence ended, Raajimera's face turned doleful. Her mark returned to its previous state, and her eyes were not glowing anymore; instead, they filled with tears. 

Tears fell down her face like an unstoppable wave. The woman didn't know whether she was sad, angry, or scared; she was so overwhelmed with emotions that she lost control of her celestial powers. The world was spinning in her eyes.

Dark clouds gathered above the village, bringing a violent sandstorm with them. A whirl of howling wind went through the village, stampeding everything in its wake.

The inhabitants began to run for their lives. The hurricane tore apart the beautiful decorations prepared for Raajimera's coronation. The destruction was merely a side effect of the powerful magic sweeping through the village, but it felt like much more than that. It was a symbol of severing her connection to the people of Duskvale. Ruined flora of what an hour ago was an oasis signified the beginning of the plight of the godforsaken.

Nobody could see what was going on; the air was the color of smoke. It was thick and suffocating. Terrified families couldn't hear each other over the roar of the raging cataclysm. 

That was the perfect opportunity for Raajimera to disappear. Forever.  
She blindly ran ahead, unsure where to go. Anywhere would be safer than Duskvale.

The woman thought she could hear them calling her name, but she didn't stop. She ran until her legs were in pain and her stomach felt like she could no longer move. Raajimera kept forcing herself to go forward until she passed out from exhaustion. 

She dropped face down into the ground. Rain began pouring from the sky, further covering her tracks. If only she could remain conscious a little longer, she would have heard the footsteps of the man who found her and felt the hands that lifted her from the mud.

The woman would later learn his name as the overwhelming darkness loosened her grip on her.

<br><br>

A group of Imperial soldiers finally arrived at Duskvale. They noticed disturbed sand where the village should be, but they didn't see the settlement itself. The situation confused them deeply.

One of them knelt and moved the sand with his fingers. He felt something under his fingertips and decided to dig for it. It was one of Raajimera's glass flowers, which must have been misplaced during the sandstorm. 

The soldier looked at it closely. He played with it and then crushed it in his hand. 

His action caused a portion of the barrier to disappear, revealing a part of the village.

"Vile trickery!" their leader shouted. "I order you to break all of these strange objects and seize whoever is inside! We will no longer tolerate their insolence."

The soldiers obeyed and took down the protective dome. They invaded the village. Blinded and injured citizens were no match for their strength; it was impossible for them to fight back…
