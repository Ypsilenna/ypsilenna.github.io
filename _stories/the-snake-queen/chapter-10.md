Many things have changed in Raajimera's life ever since she climbed that mountain. What she thought was a curse turned out to be a blessing in disguise, and what seemed terrifying at first turned out to be her safe bay.

Despite the mutual attraction she and Zephyr had for each other, both found it incredibly difficult to show their affection in front of Feng.

Raajimera felt guilty for not sharing such a big part of her current life with her scaly best friend, but she had no idea how to break the wall that blocked her. She hoped one day the animal would figure it out on its own.

Perhaps she was simply delusional, as it usually is when people learn about their own feelings.

<br>

The scorching sun shone bright above the Howling Pass. It was a hot day, yet the two decided to start their travel today. They wanted to have more time to prepare themselves before the emperor's wedding.

Raajimera helped Zephyr pack the things he decided they would need. She didn't know much about planning voyages, so she decided to simply trust him.

The man hauled some packs on the camel's back. He secured them with some leather straps to avoid losing them while in motion.

"Poor thing," Raajimera said, stroking her chin, "isn't it too much for one animal?"

Zephyr wiped the sweat off his forehead with a small cloth he had in his pocket.

"I don't think so," he answered. "He had been doing this for ages. Maybe you could ask him, but I doubt he would tell you. He's not as brilliant as Feng."

"I am not brilliant," Feng stated. "All animals can speak like I do; you just aren't equipped to hear them."

Raajimera looked at the tiny reptile and asked, "Could you ask him?"

Feng glanced at her with an offended expression.

"Can you speak with every human you meet?" the snake responded with a question. "I am a reptile. I can communicate with most snakes and occasionally lizards. I have no idea what the mammal says."

Bamieh the camel gently turned towards Feng, trying to sniff it.

"Hey, have you got any idea about personal space?!" the snake shouted angrily.

"You're the one to talk," Raajimera snorted.

"Why would you say that?" Feng asked with frustration in its voice, "I am a prime example of respecting personal space and boundaries. Tell her!"

Zephyr just looked at the cheeky reptile and said, "I am with her on this."

"Why are you two teaming up against me now?!" Feng hissed even more frustratedly.

"You licked my ear," Zephyr explained.

"Oh, I get it! You decided to become each other's best friends and leave poor Feng behind!" Feng moaned and theatrically put its tail tip on its forehead, pretending that it fainted.

The animal dropped to the ground, feigning death, yet didn't appear even remotely dead.

"A snake can't even lick people's earlobes anymore; what did the world come down to..." it complained mournfully.

"Stop being so dramatic," Raajimera said. "Zephyr, why do we need all of these things?"

"It's a long way," he answered. "We will spend many hours on the move."

The woman looked at him with an expression of a person who was deeply processing something in her head.

"If the road is so long," she said, "why did you decide to live in the Howling Pass? It's so close to Duskvale and so far away from everything else."

"Oh, I am not stuck here," he explained. "I have more camps in other parts of the empire."

"Then how did you become Zephyr of the Howling Pass and not Zephyr of the Dry Steppe or something?" she asked.

Zephyr rubbed his hands to get rid of the dust that stuck to them while he was working.

"Look," he started, "when I finally broke free, I still didn't know why my family was taken. I returned to my birthplace to stay among my people. I thought it would be safe."

He paused for a moment as if he tried to recall something.

"I had to go through a lot of trouble to reach this place; I was still a child after all. I arrived in Duskvale at night."

Zephyr looked at her.

"Nobody saw me there; I thought I was safe in the village until I heard his voice..." he said. "It was Abbas. He described to his currently deceased wife what he did in detail. He seemed to pride himself on his betrayal. They were delighted; I could see their silhouettes through his window."

"I'm so sorry, Zephyr," Raajimera said quietly.

"Fearing I would be taken again, I just ran away from there and hid in the Howling Pass," he continued. "I tried to wait until Abbas would no longer be a problem and I could return. I hoped there would still be a spot in there for me. I love that village."

"Is this why you kept an eye on him?" she asked.

"Yes," he answered. "I wanted to make sure he wouldn't do it again to somebody else... and that his life wouldn't be too easy for him after what he did."

"I find it incredibly sad that despite everything you still had hope for so long," Raajimera said. "Did you choose your new name right away?"

"Nearly," he said. "At one point I had to come to terms with the fact that I will probably never be able to go back there, but I also realized one thing."

"Which is?" Raajimera asked and raised her eyebrows.

"That I could ruin his day from the shadows and he would never see it coming." Zephyr laughed softly. The vision of Abbas losing his mind seemed to give him some strange satisfaction.

"Now that you mention this," she said, "I remember overhearing him complain about you. Although the way he spoke hinted that he doesn't actually know who you are."

The corners of Zephyr's mouth raised slightly.

"That's correct, he never saw me," he said and nodded. "He might be crafty, but he lacks the finesse required to track a man who does not desire to be found."

"He did say you are always ruining his plans..." she mentioned.

Zephyr looked at her and squinted his eyes.

"That doesn't sound like something he would say around you. Where did you hear that?" he asked.

Raajimera looked down and kicked a rock on the ground. The little stone rolled for a short while and got stuck in the sand.

"He didn't know I was around," she admitted. "I put an illusion made with Raa's magic on myself and observed him from afar. I tried to talk to him, but I nearly got caught. Feng was really angry at me that day."

Feng wiggled its tail in an annoyed manner.

"That's because I warned you and you completely ignored me and proceeded to parade around wearing other people's skins," the snake hissed unpleasantly.

"Don't be like this," Raajimera said with the tone of a beggar asking for bread. "Everything turned out right in the end."

"If you call the result of your escapade right, I don't wish to know what wrong looks like to you," Feng responded.

Zephyr observed them banter, visibly amused. They finally snapped out of it and looked at him with embarrassment in their eyes.

"I never pictured you as a spy, but knowing you have it in you has me intrigued..." he said. "What else did you learn about him?"

Raajimera brushed her chin.

"Based on his interaction with Seyed, I figured he didn't know how to read," she answered.

"That's right, he does not," Zephyr said. "I will never understand how a person with such a big aversion to literacy could also be such a sneaky scoundrel."

"That's embarrassing," Raajimera admitted.

Zephyr turned around and fixed the straps on the camel's back.

"It really is," he said, "and I don't know which one of us it stings more."

The man stretched until something cracked in his back. Raajimera shuddered, but he didn't even give her time to ask if he was alright.

"I think we are ready; shall we move?" he said without betraying even a hint of pain. She just assumed it was nothing.

"Fine," she answered. "I will put Feng in my pocket."

The little animal lifted its little snout and exclaimed cheerfully, "Actually, I will travel in **his** pocket!"

Raajimera was slightly bemused.

"What?" she asked. "Why?"

"His pockets are bigger," Feng responded while approaching Zephyr.

"You never complained about mine before," she pointed out. Raajimera did feel a little betrayed. She never thought her best friend would just leave her behind for a new pocket.

"It's because I never knew I had options!" Feng exclaimed. "On top of that, he loves me too!"

"Excuse me?" Zephyr asked sharply, "I never said I loved you."

"No, not yet," Feng responded, staring so deep into his soul that he had no choice but to put the cheeky reptile in his pocket. He couldn't believe he just lost to a creature that doesn't even have limbs.

Zephyr gently touched his temples and said, "Oh, I completely forgot about something important."

The woman looked at him curiously.

"Raajimera, sit down, please," he asked as he rummaged through all the things he had already perfectly packed.

She sat down on a boulder.

Zephyr took a small box out of one of his bags and knelt in front of her on one knee.

"We need to conceal your mark in case somebody stops us," he said as he opened the container. "It could raise suspicions, and we don't want to sound any alarms."

The man took several colored soft stones out of the box. They resembled chalk. He matched them against Raajimera's face, picked one, and saved the rest.

Zephyr cupped her face with his left hand and softly pressed the rock against her forehead. He then put it back into the box and gently smeared the line he made with his fingertips to blend it with her natural skin tone.

Raajimera was beyond delighted with his touch. He was so close to her that she could almost feel his breath. His fingers were so gentle that it reminded her of the day when he took her bandage off for the last time. If only...

Zephyr caught her stare and immediately blushed, yet didn't lose his usual composure.

"Raajimera, please," he said, "don't look at me like that—not _again_..."

"Oh, I'm sorry," she responded, completely flushed. "I didn't mean to make this awkward."

Zephyr shook his head.

"Now is not the time, that's all," he reassured her.

They stood up. Zephyr put his box back where it belonged and helped Raajimera get on the camel. This time he sat in front of her.

He gently put her hand on his stomach and said, "Hold me; I don't want you to fall."

Bamieh started going forward.

Raajimera looked back at Zephyr's house one last time. She had a stinging feeling that she would never return here again. After spending the recent weeks here, she felt more at home there than she did in Duskvale. Or, rather, she felt like home for the first time since she made the pact with Raa.

Zephyr's place wasn't the fanciest location she could ever imagine, but having him in it made all the difference. She didn't feel fully ready to let go of this chapter of her life.

Raajimera sighed softly. Not enough to alert him, but she couldn't fool herself that he wouldn't notice. The woman still felt embarrassed about the situation that happened before. She pulled her riding shawl to further cover her head and part of her face.

They traveled for what felt like hours. Zephyr didn't talk much; his eyes were focused on the horizon. Always scanning, always analyzing.

Feng peeked out of his pocket every once in a while, but finding no anchor for a conversation, it just retreated.

It gave Raajimera a long time to reflect on her situation. She didn't feel particularly comfortable facing her feelings like this.

Suddenly she could feel Zephyr's muscles tense as he saw something from a distance.

"Curses," he whispered under his breath.

Two imperial scouts on their adorned horses stopped them in their tracks. These men looked just as disturbing as the first time she saw people like them.

Both of the scouts were plain humans like Raajimera once was.

"Stop!" the taller one shouted. "Where are you headed?"

Zephyr's body language changed. He started using hand gestures and an accent that made him sound like the merchant who visited Duskvale to do business with the chieftain.

"We're going to Pale Crossing; we are but humble traders," Zephyr responded with a full range of gestures and tones.

The other scout smirked and said, "I'm afraid you will need to be detained for further investigation."

"Good gentlemen, please, let us pass!" Zephyr continued his performance with unusual levels of drama in his voice. "Our son needs medicine! His grandmother is taking care of him right now; she's going to kill me if we don't arrive on time!"

The two scouts let out a coarse laugh. Both of them looked at Raajimera with looks so vile that it made her skin crawl.

"There is a certain language you could speak to make us look away this one time," the shorter scout said. He looked repulsive when she first saw him, but now she had no words that would properly describe the intensity of the disgust harboring within her.

Raajimera felt terrified. She knew she could overpower them with Raa's magic, but she was aware that they must keep a low profile to avoid sabotaging their mission.

Zephyr reached for one of the purses he attached to the front of his camel's saddle.

"How about this?" he asked and threw it towards the men.

The taller scout grinned and responded, "I saw no traders. Let's go."

They slowly started moving when he said, "God's, how much I love the ones who pay up!"

To which the second one responded, "I think we would have a lot of fun if he didn't, though. I mean, look at his woman..."

The scouts rode away, but their laughter kept echoing in the air.

Zephyr urged the camel to move and remained silent.

His face was straight, but his muscles were tense. He took deep breaths as if he tried to calm himself down.

Feng peeked out of his pocket and cluelessly asked, "So... how much did we cost?"

"I didn't sell us," Zephyr responded with restrained anger. His voice was flat once more, but it was visible that the man was fighting his own emotions.

He remained silent until his emotions subsided.

"Raajimera, as you can see, you got to be careful around here," he finally said. "Scouts and guards aren't the sharpest tools in the shed, but things can get ugly if you don't know how to handle them."

She had no idea how to respond. Nothing in her life up to this point prepared her for this level of vile corruption that pestered the people of the empire. They were rotten at the core and apparently had no moral or ethical concerns.

The three continued their silent travel.

Raajimera pressed her cheek against Zephyr's back for comfort. She had just realized that this was the first time he didn't have a problem with her touching it.

Her thoughts were racing; she couldn't stop about the way she saw him act in front of the scouts. She could recognize his real emotions because they shared the same energy signature as the one she sensed while using Raa's divine judgment on him, but how would she see him if she didn't have magic on her side?

Zephyr was incredibly convincing. He didn't wear his heart on his sleeve, and he could quickly switch between roles without thinking twice. She was terrified to think about what he could have been if she hadn't been able to see through him, but at the same time she appreciated that he was his true self in front of her.

Suddenly Zephyr steered away from the direction they followed until now.

Raajimera's arms tightened around his waist.

"What's going on?" she asked.

"It's dawning," he answered. "I will take us to one of my secret hideouts where we can have some rest. I think we both need it."

Raajimera agreed.

They went through some rock passages off the road. The path was confusing, but Zephyr navigated it with ease. He masterfully passed what seemed like a maze, and they ended up in front of a secluded oasis.

Raajimera's eyes sparkled. The sight was idyllic.

They arrived at a wooden construction that connected directly to a cave behind it. She could see that Zephyr built this; he has this specific way of creating shelters.

The man jumped down from the camel's back and helped her reach the ground.

Raajimera stretched her legs. She could barely feel them from this many hours in the saddle.

Zephyr removed the baggage from Bamieh's back and set him free so that the animal could rest too. He then pulled sleepy Feng out of his pocket and put it on the ground.

The man approached the wooden door and began shifting some patterns to unlock them.

"What is this?" Raajimera asked.

"It's a puzzle I installed so that nobody would get in while I'm gone," he answered.

The door clicked and revealed the inner part of the hideout. It looked similar to his house, but this one only had one bed.

Raajimera followed him into the shelter. The inside was cluttered with crates and items scattered all over the place. It was much less intentional than she expected.

"I'm sorry for the mess," he said. "This time I didn't manage to clean up while you were asleep."

Raajimera laughed out loud and asked, "What do you mean? Were you just doing emergency cleaning while I was unconscious after the events in Duskvale?"

"Perhaps," he answered and looked away. His reaction gave her all the answers she needed.

Zephyr brought in all the things previously carried by Bamieh. He made some space for them and attempted not to clutter the room even more while Raajimera was observing him.

The man pulled out a red ornate blanket from the bags and showed it to Raajimera.

"Do you like it?" he asked.

The woman nodded. "Yes, why?" she asked.

"I got it at a bazaar long ago," he said. "It looks just like the one my parents gave me when I was little. I bought it to have something to remind me of the good times."

Raajimera looked at him. His face lit up when he explained. For a split second she could see the little boy he once was. She smiled.

Zephyr put the blanket on his shoulder and went out. He started a small campfire in front of the hideout.

"What is it for?" she asked.

"I thought that we could just sit here and relax," he responded. "It was a stressful day.

They wanted to put it on the ground, but as the sky darkened, the wind became chilly. The two sat very close to each other and placed the blanket on their shoulders.

Raajimera put her head on his shoulder.

"Zephyr, may I ask you about something?" she said.

His body tensed a little, but he nodded.

"Why did you tell me the truth?" she asked. "You are so talented; you could have just told me anything and I probably would be none the wiser."

He looked at the stars that started appearing in the sky.

"Growing up as I did, I learned to lie to everyone, including the people I was supposed to love," he answered. "When I first saw you, I didn't feel indifferent about you. I heard the lies they fed you, and I couldn't put you through it again. Had I lied, you wouldn't have a single person out there you could trust. I didn't want you to become resentful towards people; there are still good ones out there."

He closed his eyes for a moment and took a deep breath.

"I also knew from the beginning that you carry Raa's will. It's foolish to deceive a demigoddess," he finally added.

Raajimera knew exactly what he meant.

"Did you have anyone before me?" she asked without a hint of judgment in her voice.

"Yes, long ago," he answered. "Her name was Samira."

"What happened?" Raajimera questioned further.

"She left because I couldn't tell her the truth about me," he said. "I didn't break even when she held me against the wall. I valued my privacy and safety more than what I had with her. Now she is married to another man."

"Did you wish she would forgive you?" Raajimera asked again.

Zephyr sighed.

"It would be foolish of her to forgive me for what I did," he said. "I don't think any self-respecting person should excuse deception such as mine. She had no idea who she loved, yet I knew all about her."

He looked Raajimera in the eye and said, "I didn't know what would happen with you, but I didn't want to hurt you the way I hurt Samira."

Raajimera thought for a moment.

"So you risked everything for me knowing that we could never see each other because you didn't want to repeat a mistake of the past?" she asked.

"Yes," he responded. "I knew the price, but I would hate myself if I repeated the same mistakes."

Raajimera sought his hand under the blanket and intertwined her fingers with his.

"Thank you," she said.

They looked at each other and leaned in for a tender kiss when they were suddenly mortified to notice that Feng had been watching them all along.

"You didn't tell me you accepted him as your mate," Feng said with an accusatory tone. "I mean, I noticed that something was amiss, but I thought you would tell me!"

"I'm sorry," Raajimera responded, visibly embarrassed. "Are you upset with me?"

Feng shook its head.

"Not at all. I am happy when you're happy," it said, "just communicate with me next time."

Raajimera nodded.

The small reptile looked at her and demanded, "Now let me under the blanket too for a moment! I am cold in here!"

They laughed and put Feng between them.

The three spent some time stargazing in complete silence. It was just them, the fire, and the universe.

When the flame began to die, Zephyr and Raajimera decided to move into the hideout. The man put the blanket on his shoulder and noticed that Feng wasn't following them.

"We are going inside. Are you coming with us?" he asked.

"No," Feng answered while staring into the abyss. "I will stay here a bit longer to contemplate the meaning of life."

"As you wish," Zephyr said. "You can crawl back under the door when you need."

Raajimera disappeared in the building. Zephyr closed the door behind him and spread his blanket on top of the bed.

Raajimera sat down on its edge while Zephyr looked for something in his stash.

He pulled out a beautiful oil lamp and lit it up to set the mood for a lovely late-night discussion.

They ended up not having a conversation.

<br><br>


Zephyr woke up early in the morning. Raajimera was still asleep with her head on his bare chest. He couldn't fall back asleep, so he decided to gently leave the bed without waking her up.

He collected his clothes from the ground and put them back on his body.

The man took a bucket and a small net and left the hideout.

He took a fresh morning breath. The sun was just beginning its journey in the sky.

Zephyr started walking towards the oasis. It looked like a lake surrounded with vivid vegetation. It was beautiful compared to the rest of the landscape.

Feng noticed him from a distance and decided to investigate the mysterious behavior. The animal followed the man as he began disappearing behind rocks and plants.

"Where are you going?" the snake asked. "I will go with you."

Zephyr stopped and looked at Feng.

"I am just going to collect some food," he answered. "Shouldn't you stay with Raajimera? She might be confused when she wakes up and notices that she is all alone."

"She likes to sleep a lot," the animal said. "She won't wake up early unless you tell her to do so, which you didn't."

"Why do you want to go with me?" Zephyr asked. "You don't even have arms to help me carry the things on our way back. Do you not trust me?"

Feng looked at him and tilted its head.

"I just want to spend some quality time with my new best friend!" it blurted out. "Get to know each other... maybe find out how you learned to speak my language..."

"So this is what it's all about!" Zephyr shouted and resumed walking.

The little snake desperately tried to catch up with the man who now doubled his walking speed.

"Come on! I need to know," Feng pleaded.

"No," Zephyr answered firmly.

"Please!" the animal kept begging. "I know that it's possible for a champion to become overloaded with divine energy from strong emotions and then transfer some abilities to somebody else, but I can't imagine how it happened in your case."

Zephyr stopped abruptly. His hand tightened around the bucket's handle.

"It was..." he said, "...an accident."

"While doing what?" Feng was not getting the clue.

Zephyr remained silent. He tried to keep a straight face.

The man approached the edge of the water and placed his equipment on the ground.

"Did she get angry at you?" Feng kept asking. "But that wouldn't make any sense because you would have to be within the range of her seal for it to work."

"Please, no more questions," he said as he filled the bucket with some water.

"Why don't you want to tell me?" Feng asked.

"Because it's embarrassing," Zephyr responded.

Suddenly a spark of thought raced through Feng's face, and a sudden realization hit it like a rock fired from a slingshot.

"You didn't make her angry," it said. "It was the opposite, wasn't it?"

Zephyr tried to untangle his net while mumbling something under his breath with his face colored like a spring blossom.

"But if it was good," Feng said, "then why don't you want to talk about it?"

"Because it's personal," he responded.

"Oh, alright," the animal said and stuck its tongue out. "How did you end up touching her forehead?"

Zephyr shook his head and said, "I didn't. She just leaned towards me and pressed her head against mine. I think she wanted to show her affection this way, but it was quite painful, to be fair."

Feng made a very silly face.

"Oh, oh, OH!" it exclaimed. "She gave you an electric headache!"

"You can call it like that," Zephyr agreed.

The man sat on the ground with the small net still in his hand.

"If you knew this would happen," Feng said, "would you still get yourself in that situation?"

Zephyr glanced at the animal with a slightly warmer look in his eyes.

"I would endure all the headaches for her," he finally said.

"That's sweet," Feng admitted, "and slightly disturbing."

"Perhaps," Zephyr said, "but we have one thing in common, she and I. For both of us the only way left is forward."

Feng's face brightened like a little sunshine.

"See?" it said. "This is why you're my new best friend!"

"What about Raajimera?" Zephyr asked and raised his eyebrows.

"I can have more than one!" Feng explained.

The man couldn't argue with the snake's logic. He silently cast his net into the water of the oasis, and they both waited.

Some time passed in total silence. He finally pulled his net out of the water and saw that some fish got caught in it. It was not too many, but it was enough for them to eat.

Zephyr carefully moved the fish to the bucket.

"Oh, fish!" Feng exclaimed, "I want one!"

The reptile put its head in the bucket, but Zephyr pulled it back.

"Don't touch these," he said. "I will catch one just for you."

He crouched at the water's edge and remained very still for several minutes. When a small fish finally came close, he quickly reached in and grabbed it with his hands.

Zephyr returned to Feng and placed the fish on the ground.

The snake was overjoyed and devoured the fish in one gulp.

Zephyr spread his net on a boulder to dry it in the sun and sat down on the shore. He enjoyed the light breeze hitting his face.

Feng, whose belly was now comically distorted from its meal, slithered next to him.

"You seemed scary at first, but now I can see that you are pretty lovely," the animal said and put its snout on his lap.

Zephyr looked down at the snake and responded, "That was a compliment, I suppose."

Feng yawned.

"Why are you wearing that hood all the time?" it asked.

"It makes me feel less exposed," he explained.

"Before Raa dispatched me, she mentioned that we could meet a kind man who would guide us," Feng recalled. "I imagined you would be different, though."

"Different how?" Zephyr asked and scratched his face.

"I pictured you with long blond hair flowing in the wind..." Feng began describing. "...and with a very wide and hairy upper body. At least I got the chest hair part right. You still look terrific, though!"

Zephyr had no idea what to do with that information.

"...thank you?" he finally mumbled.

The man couldn't believe that he was capable of resisting torture and fooling every imperial bastard on his way, yet there was this little critter that bypassed all of his walls with sheer audacity.

When the fishing net was dry, Zephyr collected it, grabbed his bucket, and headed back.

"Let's go," he said.

Feng followed him joyfully.

The man took the snake from the ground and put it on his shoulder.

Slowly approaching the hideout, they saw Raajimera outside. She was there bending the surrounding sand in the air. The demigoddess was making it dance above her, swirl in patterns that resembled flowers. She had the same sense of wonder painted on her face as she did when she first learned how to use this power.

Feng opened its mouth to shout her name, but Zephyr covered its face with his hand. They just stood in the distance observing her.

The absolute joy she expressed made Zephyr so happy. She hasn't used any of her powers ever since he brought her from the road to Duskvale. Raajimera seemed to have repressed this part of her after she was attacked by Seyed.

Observing the dancing sand made him realize how much she had recovered since he started to take care of her. She seemed genuinely happy, and he was all there for it.

Raajimera twirled and suddenly noticed them. She stopped what she was doing and waved both her hands.

Zephyr smiled warmly and walked towards her.

Maybe they won't fix the whole world in one day. That's an impossible ask, but for now things get better for the microcosm between their arms, and that's enough.

Slow and steady, one smile at a time.
