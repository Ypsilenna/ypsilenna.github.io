The empire of Raajimera prospered for many years; however, all that rose must once fall. The decline happened naturally, over the course of years. Plagues and natural disasters made some people disappear; some others simply relocated. There were certain things not even gods could protect them from.

No matter how hard Raajimera tried, she never managed to extend the lifespan of Zephyr and Feng in a meaningful way. Although they did stay around for longer than they normally would, nothing can escape the vicious circle of life.

Raajimera was married multiple times over the course of her life, but she only ever loved her first husband, about whom she never let her people forget. Holding on to his memory, she mourned him in front of their statue in the center of the capital city until ruthless time took even that from her.

Her story ended just how it started, with a nameless woman atop a mountain evoking a goddess in the sky.

"Raa!" Raajimera shouted, her legs twitching from the long walk she took to get there. Her body was covered with rags that once were her imperial silks. "I served you for so many years; why did you do this to me? Everyone I loved, everything I had... gone..."

Raa slowly appeared in the sky and looked at her herald.

"The world has no use for either of us any longer," the goddess said. "We have outstayed our welcome."

"What am I supposed to do?" Raajimera asked.

"You were my best servant for so long. I want to offer you a gift," Raa said. "I can relieve you of your service. You would get a chance to live as a regular human in this new world for the next fifty years; then your journey would end."

Raa paused for a moment.

"You could also accompany me as I leave this plane and ascend into the Eternal Gardens to spend the rest of eternity surrounded by those we loved in life."

Raajimera's eyes sparkled.

"I could see Zephyr and Feng again, after all this time..." she said. "Wouldn't I sound ungrateful if I chose one of these fates? You made me perfect..."

Raa shook her head.

"I just gave you a blank canvas; you painted this masterpiece yourself," the goddess responded.

"Why did you do it, though?"

"You had potential, but you were held back by the armor you built out of your childhood pain," Raa said. "You were just a young girl given up by her parents who always assured you that you were special. You turned cold and ambitious to ensure nobody would harm you again. That was when you turned to me. I looked deep in your soul and saw that you had the prospect to embody the qualities I sought."

Raajimera looked at her slightly confused.

"Justice without compassion is cruelty, but compassion without cold judgment is foolishness." Raa said. "I needed a champion capable of both to fully carry out my will. The seed of compassion was already within you; you just had to find your way back to it. It wouldn't be possible if you kept your memories."

"I understand," Raajimera answered. "I am ready to choose my fate."

"Which one is it?"

"I want to go to the Eternal Gardens with you. There is nothing in this world that I haven't already seen, and the pain left from losing my loved ones never ceased to burn in my heart."

Raa nodded.

"Let's move, then, my beloved daughter," she responded, extending the tip of her tail towards Raajimera.

The woman touched it with her fingers.

They both began ascending into the sky until their spirits departed and their bodies turned into stardust, forever remaining suspended in the night sky as the constellation of two mothers, the mother of snakes and the mother of the new world.<br><br>
