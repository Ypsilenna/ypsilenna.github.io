The memory of the previous emperor slowly began fading away as the original red banners were replaced with dark blue ones embroidered with golden snake symbols. It was merely a start of the monumental transformation process. The new leader knew that the future held a lot in her cards, yet for now, she decided to focus on establishing herself in this new world.

The day was coming to an end as God-Empress walked to the middle of the greater platform in front of the palace. She was wearing a red robe with long sleeves. Her entire outfit was adorned with little golden floral patterns that matched the jewelry she was wearing. The only thing that was still missing was a veil on her head.

Raajimera looked around to confirm that she was alone. All she could hear was silence occasionally interrupted by echoing sounds of the kitchen staff preparing food for the ceremony. The guests haven't arrived yet.

The garden was lush for this part of the continent. It was dotted with small candles flickering gently like fireflies in the setting sun.

Raajimera took a deep breath and raised her hands up.

"Raa, Spirit of Dusk, I call upon you," she exclaimed.

The orange clouds softly shifted hues as Twilight Mother appeared in the sky.

The goddess looked at her herald with a slightly confused expression.

"Why do you summon me?" Raa asked.

"I want you to attend my wedding," the woman responded.

"I don't understand your human ceremonies," the deity said, measuring her with her eyes.

"I am aware," Raajimera answered, "but I don't know my family. I hoped you would be around as my mother."

The face of Raa shifted into a softer expression; she seemed to be smiling softly.

"I am honored," she said. "I will attend."

Their tender moment was abruptly interrupted by a group of servants running towards Raajimera.

"Empress!" one woman shouted, trying to catch her breath. "Mister Zephyr cast us out of the chamber and locked himself inside."

Raajimera raised her eyebrows.

"Why did he do that?" she asked.

"He said we did everything wrong and he will just get dressed on his own," one young servant explained.

"I will talk to him; go back inside."

The servants left.

Raajimera tried to follow them when visibly agitated Feng stopped her.

"Can you tell me why the stinky camel is here?" Feng hissed aggressively. "I thought animals don't attend parties."

"Feng, you are here too," Raajimera pointed out.

"But I am a divine messenger! That's different."

Raajimera sighed.

"Be kind to Bamieh," she said. "He means a lot to Zephyr."

Feng scoffed.

"Hey, where are you going? I am not done with you!" it kept shouting.

"I'm sorry, but I got something important to take care of," Raajimera said as she moved towards the palace. "Don't harass Bamieh."

Feng looked at the camel from a distance and mumbled, "Fine, but don't think we are friends."

Raajimera found herself in front of the chamber the servants told her about. The workers were waiting for her, but they were too scared to come to the door.

"Zephyr, the servants told me you removed them from the room and locked yourself in there. What is going on?" she asked.

The man was sitting on a divan and battling a long piece of cloth.

"It's because these idiots don't know how to wrap a turban!" he shouted with anger in his voice.

"Why do you think so?" she asked.

Zephyr let out a frustrated chain of profanities.

Raajimera sat on the floor and rested her back against the door.

"Zephyr, what is really going on?"

The man sighed heavily and stopped trying to tame the fabric.

After a pause, he finally said, "I am sorry. I just have nowhere to hide now. This new life is overwhelming."

"I hear you. Do you want me to come in?" she asked and pressed her cheek against the cold wood.

"No, I shouldn't see you before the ceremony," Zephyr responded sharply.

"Could you let them in so that they can help you get dressed?"

Raajimera waited in silence until she heard the door unlock.

As the servants entered the chamber, Raajimera caught a quick glimpse of a familiar face. It was difficult for her to remember all of these new people, but a sudden realization hit her. One of them was Mehri, the woman who weeks ago was meant to marry the previous emperor.

The poor lady had nowhere to go after the coup. Knowing that sooner or later she would be sacrificed to the gods of the Underworld, she felt grateful to Raajimera for saving her from the fate of being the emperor's wife.

Mehri wanted to repay her saviors by joining the palace staff. Only this time she was in this building of her own free will, and she felt confident that everything would be alright.

Raajimera felt sorry that she couldn't offer her a better position, but Mehri was adamant.

Zephyr finally stopped fuming, so the empress just walked away hoping for the best. She went down the stairs and looked around. The place seemed immaculate, which made her feel like no detail would escape her attention, and yet she found a small concealed space she never noticed before. Just behind the staircase.

Raajimera sat there and observed the corridor from the hiding spot. She couldn't stop thinking about Zephyr's recent behavior. The poor man held it together for his entire life, but now that he had nowhere to hide, his whole act crumbled down like a house of cards.

The woman wondered whether he would be able to live there with her for good. She felt partially responsible for his distress, but he made a choice and she didn't want to take it away from him.

Raajimera noticed that the guests began arriving. She didn't know any of these people. Zephyr invited the most influential and established personalities of the capital city. They needed to fill the ballroom somehow, and none of them had living relatives they would know of.

She felt like she underestimated Zephyr's effectiveness as a spy, not fully realizing that he collected intel on all of these people prior to meeting her.

The woman waited for the right moment and sneaked out of her hiding spot to return to her preparations. The servants put a veil on her head and pinned it down with an ornate headpiece.

Her face was now buried under a semitransparent fabric. She wondered how she would get to the ballroom on her own with this thing in front of her eyes.

Suddenly Feng slithered through the ajar door.

It looked at her and said, "You look just like me when I molt."

"Very funny," Raajimera responded without laughing. "Can you guide me? I shouldn't remove it from my face."

"You could just lift it up and pretend you never touched it," Feng pointed out.

"Fine, I will do that, and you can go back to your disagreement with Bamieh."

"That was uncalled for," Feng responded and crawled on her shoulder. The animal lifted the veil with its tail. "I will hold this thing for you so that nobody can accuse you of tampering with it."

They headed downstairs towards the ballroom. They crossed the corridor in complete silence until they reached the end. Feng let go of her veil and perched on her shoulder, wrapping its tail around her upper arm.

Two guards opened the heavy door for Raajimera, and she entered the ballroom. All eyes turned on her.

Zephyr was already waiting for her, sitting cross-legged on an ornate cushion in front of a ceremonial spread.

Raajimera sat on his left. Feng gently leaned out from her shoulder and lifted the veil with its tail. For a moment it was just Zephyr and her as their eyes met in the tall oval mirror in front of them. They were captured in the frame, sitting still like a beautiful painting suspended between the two lit candelabras placed on both sides of the mirror.

The woman focused on her future husband's appearance. His body was wrapped in a white robe with a line of golden geometric shapes embroidered along his silhouette. The servants seemed to have colored his already dark eyelids with a dash of black eye shadow. Raajimera found his olive green turban and garlands of golden jewelry excessive, but she knew how much he valued wearing clothing appropriate to the situation.

Raajimera's eyes moved down to the spread. It was suspended on a small wooden platform in front of them. All the traditional components made it appear busy, although they were perfectly spaced. There was an old poetry book right in front of the mirror. Both sides of the spread were adorned with flowers, fruit, spices, and decorative eggs like a beautiful frame.

A ceremonial cloth flashed in the mirror as the royal servants spread it above them. The sound of grinding sugar cones followed it, bringing sweet blessings from above. Raajimera noticed Mehri among the celebrating servants with a smile on her face she had not expected to see several weeks ago.

The wedding officiant approached them and began reading poetry for the guests sitting in front of them. Raajimera didn't pay too much attention to it. The sound of the music and the colors of the decorations overwhelmed her, clashing in her head like a wave at the sea.

The man finally closed the book and turned towards the couple.

"Do you wish to accept Empress Raajimera as your wife?" he asked.

"Yes," Zephyr answered without elaborating needlessly.

The officiant turned to her and asked, "Do you wish to accept Zephyr of the Howling Pass as your husband?"

Raajimera looked at him without saying a word. A sudden thought appeared on the back of her head.

"The bride is out picking flowers!" someone shouted across the ballroom.

The officiant repeated the question.

Still without a word, Raajimera was lost in her thoughts. She quietly wished for a miracle that her biological family would have somehow recognized her and attended the wedding. It would be a welcome surprise, although she knew that even they couldn't resist Raa's magic.

"The bride is putting the flowers in a vase," another guest shouted.

Finally, the man repeated the question for the third time.

Raajimera looked at him, then her eyes wandered to the tall window behind him, where she could see the goddess observe her through the glass.

"With the permission of Raa, yes," she responded.

All guests began cheering for the couple. The servants put the sugar cones and the cloth back on the spread.

Zephyr grabbed a jar of honey from the little platform and dipped his little finger in it. He leaned closer to Raajimera and pressed the golden liquid against her lips, allowing her to taste it. She mirrored his actions, and they looked each other deep in the eye. Zephyr moved so close to her that she could feel his breath on her cheek.

The woman closed her eyes, and he kissed her. Zephyr smelled like a lush meadow and tasted like sugar.

A sudden salve of cheering voices pierced the air around them. The guests clapped and congratulated them loudly.

Raajimera and Zephyr got up and walked towards them. The guests lavished them with presents, but the couple decided to take a closer look after the ceremony.

They headed towards the exit. The guests showered them with coins and flower petals as they walked.

Raa already waited for them in the sky above the door.

"I'm proud of you," she said, "and I don't mean just Raajimera."

Zephyr tightened his hand around his wife's arm and smiled softly.

"Thank you," he responded. Never would he have thought this day would happen to him.

The couple led their guests into the garden where the feast was already prepared. Raajimera and Zephyr took a seat on a blanket in the middle of the reception. The air was pleasantly chill.

Zephyr placed an empty plate on his right side and put his palms together.

Raajimera looked at him and asked, "Who is this spot for?"

After a brief pause, he glanced at her and said, "My parents. This is where they would sit if they were still alive."

Raajimera nodded. She suddenly realized that she wasn't the only person wishing her family could make it to the party. It made her uncomfortable because it was so obvious he would feel like this and yet she was too focused on her own pain to notice his.

The woman grabbed another plate and put it on her left side.

"Mine would sit here," she finally said.

They looked at one another and understood each other without words. He gently stroked her cheek with his finger. She grabbed his wrist and pressed his hand more against her face.

People say that eyes are the mirror of the soul. For Raajimera and Zephyr, it felt more like looking into the surface of an endless sea, each of them on the opposite side, touching each other through the ever-changing currents of the tide.

A sudden movement slipped through the corner of their eyes and made them snap out of the spell they had put each other under. They were chilled.

"FENG, NO!" Raajimera shouted as she launched herself towards the wine jug in front of them.

"I want to try it!" Feng calmly stated with a muffled voice, as it already was partially in the container.

She tried to pull Feng out of the jug. The animal hung upside down for a second and attempted to re-enter the jug. Zephyr quickly put a plate on top of the container.

"You can't try it," he said. "It's not good for snakes."

"It's not good for you either, and here we are," Feng answered, visibly insulted, then attempted to slither under the plate.

"One would think that being able to communicate with animals would make them understand you," Zephyr mumbled under his breath.

"An animal is an animal no matter how many words it learns," Feng stated. "Don't let common language fool you."

"Feng, please," Raajimera said. "No wine. You may have something else."

"Why not?"

"Because I am worried you would fall inside it and drown," she answered.

"Ah, you are worried about my safety!" Feng beamed. "Mister Grumpy should have led with that."

The animal moved away from the container and watched the servants bring plates of food. Feng snatched a small piece of meat and dragged it off triumphantly before devouring it.

Raajimera observed her little friend fitting the meal in its mouth with amusement.

The woman looked around. Their guests seemed to be having a good time, yet she did feel out of place among them. She watched the nobles and the ministers eat and chatter joyously, yet she couldn't bring herself to speak with them.

The couple put some food on their plates. Raajimera took roasted lamb with flatbread and vegetables. Zephyr did the same. She wanted to comment on that, but he was eating in complete silence.

Raajimera felt awkward. They always talked while eating. It was like a ritual where the main course was the content of their souls while the food was just a side dish. His abrupt silence made her feel even more isolated.

The woman was alone with her thoughts and the mildly appetizing lamb meat when Mehri brought her some desserts.

"Is everything alright, Empress?" she asked.

Raajimera looked at her and said, "Yes, don't worry."

"We have a lot of work now, but call me if you need anything," Mehri responded and walked away.

The empress put her plate away and grabbed another one to try the desserts. She ended up eating some baklava. It was cut in small diamond shapes and decorated with rose petals.

Raajimera expected it to be sweeter, but it had more almonds than syrup, so it was easier for her to eat. It did, however, make her fingers sticky.

She quickly wiped them with a handkerchief with the intention of grabbing another snack next when she saw Zephyr get up without a word and storm away.

"Where are you going?" she asked, but he didn't respond.

Raajimera followed him to the empty ballroom and closed the door behind them. It was the first time she realized how much noise their guests made. She felt like she could breathe again.

The man sat down on a sofa in the corner and buried his face in his hands.

"Zephyr, what happened?" she asked.

"I can't."

"You can't what?"

Raajimera took the place next to him.

"I feel so exposed," he said. "Being invisible was easy. Now everyone knows me and knows where I am at all times. I can't stand the pressure."

The woman put her hand on his upper back, unsure how to react. She feared the worst.

"This new life is so noisy. I have to manage so many things I never thought about," he continued. "And this wedding is just for the people to see us. All this crowd, all this noise..."

"Zephyr, look at me," she requested and gently moved his face up with her hand. "You are free to return to the Howling Pass or any other of your hideouts whenever you please, rest from the burdens of this path, and return to me when you are ready."

Raajimera put her other hand on his lap.

"This wedding is for the people, that's right," she continued, "but I really meant it when I said I wanted us to get married. Nobody can hear us now, and I will still say it. Yes, I will marry you. I will go anywhere for you and, if gods permit, even seek you on the other side, because there is no other flame burning as bright as my feelings for you. I love you."

Zephyr looked at her, and a small tear fell down his cheek.

"I love you too, my yasamin," he said as he grabbed both her hands. 

After a brief moment of silence, he added, "I'm so sorry for the way I behaved today."

Raajimera shook her head.

"Don't be," she said. "I know how difficult this situation is for you. I just want to make sure you won't give up after everything we've been through."

"I will not," he said with a shaky voice. "Staying with you was the most important choice I ever made, and I will stand by that forever."

Zephyr kissed Raajimera softly and gently hugged her, pressing her body closer to his chest. She wrapped her arms around his neck.

They spent a moment just cuddling on the sofa until Zephyr broke the silence.

"I think we should return to the party," he said. "They may be unhappy if we stayed here."

"Are you ready to step back into the noise?" she asked.

"I will manage," he responded and got up from the sofa.

Raajimera followed him to the door, and they opened it together. Their eyes met the sight of Feng surrounded by guests. The visitors were looking at the little snake in awe. Everyone tried to hold the pocket-sized divine messenger.

Feng noticed Zephyr and Raajimera from a distance.

"Look, I'm famous!" it shouted.

Raajimera giggled and grabbed Zephyr's hand, pulling him towards the heart of the party. They were about to dance when they glanced up and saw Raa bouncing her head joyfully.

"What are you doing?" Raajimera asked.

Raa looked down and responded, "I'm attending the party as you requested."

"Are you enjoying the music?"

"It's delightful," Raa said and continued bobbing her head.

Raajimera lowered her voice.

"I wonder if they noticed," she said.

"I think they had too much wine to be particularly bothered," Zephyr said. "Speaking of which, would you like to try some?"

"No," she responded. "I don't like bitter drinks."

"Oh, it seems like you tried the wine of Duskvale. This one is sweet and fragrant. I could pour some in my cup and let you try if you wish."

Raajimera nodded.

Zephyr put some wine in a chalice and passed it to her. She hesitantly took a sip. The wine was very light and smelled like roses.

"Oh, now I can imagine why they would drink too much of this," she commented.

"This is why they serve this one on long parties," Zephyr said. "You aren't supposed to drink more than a few sips of the wine from Duskvale."

Raajimera looked at him, visibly surprised.

"Why not?"

"Because," he started, "they make it far too strong for what it's supposed to be, and drinking too much could make you sick or cause you to black out. Didn't they tell you it was dangerous?"

The woman suddenly realized that the first time her life was saved was not by Raa's snakes but by her own tongue. She shook her head with a grimace.

Zephyr understood her facial expression.

"This one is safe," he said. "Let's just sit down and enjoy the rest of the night."

The man poured some wine into another chalice, and they took a seat on the ground. Once more they noticed how beautiful the sky was as the night fell. The garden lights mixed with the stars, making them feel like they were suspended in space.

Raajimera finished her drink and curled up next to Zephyr. He kissed one of her temples, and they just spent the rest of the night trying to relax.

With each hour passing, more and more guests headed home. Early in the morning Zephyr and Raajimera followed the last noble to the exit. The party was finally over. Zephyr breathed a sigh of relief.

"The guests are gone at last," he said.

Raajimera wanted to respond when she saw a group of commoners behind the main gate. They seemed impoverished and starved. Their eyes were longing for her attention.

She walked towards the gate.

"Are you hungry?" she asked. "We still have a lot of food left from the reception if you're in need."

Raajimera could see that they were too embarrassed and proud to admit it. She grabbed the bars of the gate and pulled them with all her might to open it.

"Please, walk in," she said. "The feast is in the garden; just take the road to the left, and you shall see it."

The commoners shyly entered the garden. Their expressions made them seem like they don't feel worthy of hospitality.

"Go ahead. You are welcome here," she said.

Zephyr approached her once the last commoner disappeared into the garden.

"I apologize, Zephyr. I just felt so sorry for them," she said.

He laughed shortly. "Raajimera, you are too good for this world. I just hoped we got rid of all the guests."

"You can rest if you please; I will stay with them."

Zephyr shook his head.

"No, my love," he said. "I will stay with you. I already made that choice."

<hr>

The empire was never the same from that day onward. It doesn't mean that things were always easy for Raajimera. The empress and her consort spent long years trying to iron out the rough edges. The previous emperor's corruption ran deep in a way that no amount of goodwill would ever cancel it out, but it didn't stop them from trying.

Zephyr periodically sneaked out of the capital city in the veil of the night to ease his nerves, but he always returned to his nest like a swallow in spring. His new position forced him to abandon his old life of a spy, yet it couldn't stop him from longing for it. Heeding the call from the shadows, he established an imperial guild he called Sons of the Wind in which he personally trained entire generations of new operatives. Zephyr wanted to make sure that Raajimera would always have their help even if one day he was no longer around.

Step by step, the empire became a place worth living in. Even though it was never perfect, Raajimera and Zephyr made it as close as it got to paradise, and that's all that mattered to them.

Those who enjoy good endings prefer to place the final period here. After all, it was the perfect ending to the story of a woman who craved power and earned more than she asked for. However, those who are not afraid to dig know that the end of her story came much later...
